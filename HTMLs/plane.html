One cold summer dawn, stirring in bed,

through the window I saw an airplane weeping.



'You alright...?' 　I asked.



'I once had a mom and dad, but it's so sad

how they didn't have each other..

What keeps you awake this late?'    asked the airplane.



'Few months ago I went to a coffee shop,

asked for a drink with the strongest caffeine.'  I replied.

'They gave me a shot of loneliness, and

I've barely been able to sleep ever since.'



'Oh, those shots make up my fuel.

They pump them into me in gallons. That's why I never sleep.

Let me know next time you can't fall asleep,

I'm always down for a nice quiet midnight walk.'

he spoke, as he flew away.
